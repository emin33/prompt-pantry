# Pasta e Fagioli — The Research Build

*A from-scratch synthesis of the best techniques from Marcella Hazan, America's Test Kitchen, Lidia Bastianich, Ina Garten, and Italian regional tradition. Thick, stew-like, deeply savory — the way it's supposed to be.*

**Servings:** 6  
**Active Time:** ~45 minutes  
**Total Time:** ~2.5 hours (plus overnight brine)

---

## Ingredients

### Beans
- 1 lb dried cannellini beans
- 1½ tbsp kosher salt (for brine)
- 8 cups water (for brine)

### Soup Base
- 6 oz pancetta, diced (¼-inch)
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 yellow onion, finely diced
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced
- 1 carrot, finely diced
- 3 anchovy fillets, minced
- 5 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- ½ tsp red pepper flakes
- 1 fresh rosemary sprig
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 Parmesan rind (3–4 inch piece)
- 3 cups chicken stock
- 2 cups reserved bean cooking liquid

### Pasta
- 8 oz ditalini pasta

### Finishing
- ½ cup Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated
- 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
- ¼ cup fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- 3 tbsp finishing extra-virgin olive oil (best you have)
- ½ lemon (for squeezing)
- 1 tsp black pepper
- Kosher salt to taste

---

## Instructions

### 1. Brine the Beans Overnight
Dissolve 1½ tbsp kosher salt in 8 cups water in a large bowl. Add the dried cannellini beans and soak at room temperature for 8–12 hours (or overnight). This brine softens the skins and seasons the beans through to the center.

### 2. Cook the Beans from Scratch
Drain and rinse the brined beans. Place them in a large pot, cover with fresh water by 2 inches. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook uncovered for 45–60 minutes until beans are creamy and tender but not falling apart. Skim any foam that rises. Season with a pinch of salt halfway through. When done, **reserve 2 cups of the starchy bean cooking liquid** — this is liquid gold. Drain the beans.

### 3. Render the Pancetta
In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot over medium heat, add 2 tbsp olive oil and the diced pancetta. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the pancetta is golden and has rendered its fat — about 6–8 minutes. Do not drain the fat; it's your soffritto cooking medium.

### 4. Build the Soffritto
Add the onion, celery, and carrot to the pot with the rendered pancetta fat. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are soft and translucent — about 8 minutes. Clear a small space in the center of the pot and add the minced anchovy fillets. Press them into the hot surface and stir until they dissolve into the vegetables — about 30 seconds. This is your invisible umami layer.

### 5. Toast the Garlic and Tomato Paste
Add the garlic and stir for 30 seconds until fragrant — do not let it brown. Add the tomato paste and red pepper flakes. Stir the paste into the vegetables and cook for 1–2 minutes until it darkens slightly and smells sweet. This Maillard reaction on the paste concentrates the tomato flavor.

### 6. Purée One-Third of the Beans
Take roughly one-third of your cooked cannellini beans and add them to a blender or food processor with about 1 cup of reserved bean cooking liquid. Blend until completely smooth. Alternatively, you can mash them directly in the pot against the side with a wooden spoon — the traditional way.

### 7. Combine and Simmer
Add the whole beans and the bean purée to the Dutch oven. Pour in the chicken stock and remaining bean cooking liquid. Add the rosemary sprig, bay leaves, and Parmesan rind. Stir everything together, bring to a gentle simmer, and cook uncovered for 25–30 minutes. The soup should reduce and thicken. Stir occasionally and watch the bottom — bean purée can stick. If it gets too thick, add splashes of hot water. The target consistency is thick and stew-like. Season with kosher salt as needed throughout.

### 8. Cook the Pasta Separately
While the soup simmers, bring a pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Cook the ditalini for 1–2 minutes LESS than the package direction — you want it slightly underdone because it will continue softening in the hot soup. Drain, toss with a tiny drizzle of olive oil to prevent sticking, and set aside.

### 9. Finish and Season
Remove and discard the rosemary sprig, bay leaves, and Parmesan rind. Take the pot off heat. Stir in the grated Parmigiano-Reggiano until melted and incorporated. Add the red wine vinegar — this is the brightness trick. Squeeze in the lemon juice. Season with black pepper and additional salt to taste. Let the soup rest off heat for 5–10 minutes before serving. It thickens as it sits — this is what you want.

### 10. Serve
Divide the ditalini among bowls. Ladle the thick bean soup over the pasta. Drizzle each bowl generously with your best extra-virgin olive oil — this is non-negotiable. Top with chopped parsley and more grated Parmigiano. Pass red pepper flakes at the table.

## Notes

**Leftover strategy:** Store the bean soup base and cooked pasta SEPARATELY in the fridge. The base keeps 3–5 days and actually improves overnight (the Neapolitans call the next-day version *arrepusata* and consider it superior). Reheat the base with splashes of hot water or stock — it will have thickened considerably. Cook fresh pasta per portion if you want peak texture. The base (without pasta) freezes beautifully for up to 3 months.

**Bean sourcing:** If you can find dried borlotti (cranberry) beans at an Italian market or online, use them — they're the most traditional choice and produce an even creamier, nuttier result. Rancho Gordo is a reliable mail-order source.

**The anchovy question:** Trust it. The 3 fillets dissolve completely during the soffritto and contribute only deep savory flavor — no one will detect fish. This is ATK's most impactful move.

**Scaling up:** This recipe reheats so well that doubling is strongly recommended. Future you will be grateful.

**Expansion pack techniques worth trying on a future batch:**
- **Marcella Hazan's seared spare ribs** — brown them in the Dutch oven before the pancetta, simmer in the soup, pull before serving.
- **White wine deglaze** — splash of dry white wine after the tomato paste step, cook until evaporated.
